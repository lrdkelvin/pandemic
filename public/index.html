FAQ:


What is growth rate?

Growth rate is the exponential multiplier for the disease. It is calculated by comparing the value of NEW cases and deaths between today and yesterday. For example, if there were 50 new cases yesterday and 50 new cases today. That day has a growth factor of 1. If there were 50 new cases yesterday and 100 new cases today, there is a growth rate of 2. A consistent growth fact of anything over 1 suggests that more and more people are getting infected, which might suggest exponential growth. If we stay consistenly below 1, the disease is losing and we are winning.

Why are the growth rates for weeks soo much higher?

The time is increased, so the rate increases. If you had 1000 new cases this time last week, and today you have 6000 new cases, its a six -fold increase! Going back a couple weeks to calculate growth rates over week long periods takes us back to mid-march, right when mass production of tests was coming out and confirmed cases grew rapidly. Expect these numbers to drop and normalize over time.

Okay, so why have the weekly growth rate if it's scarier?

The point is to show overall trends. You can see that the numbers for this week are so much better than last week. In other words, daily growth rates fluctuate like crazy, so if you can see that thing are improving overall, this suggests we are getting closer to life as usual. Once those weekly numbers dip below 1, I expect restrictions to start being lifted.

How are predictions calculated?

I wish it was smarter, but I simply apply the days growth rate to the new cases and that gives me a prediction for tomorrow. Unless the prediction is crazy (due to massive daily fluctuations), they provide a small look into the future.
